### Solution description and output

**Q1a: Briefly describe your solution to solve the marine litter challenge. Explain the project objective, value proposition and intended activities.**

RePlas combats Nigeria's marine plastic crisis through blockchain-powered tokenization, turning waste into valuable digital assets.

**Objective:** Reduce plastic flow into marine ecosystems via scalable, transparent collection incentives.

**Value Proposition:**
1. **Environmental:** Prevent ocean plastic through source collection incentives
2. **Social:** Provide sustainable income for waste pickers via digital tokens and financial inclusion
3. **Economic:** Create transparent recycled plastic supply chain for businesses and impact investors

**Activities:**
• Deploy blockchain platform for waste tracking/tokenization
• Establish collection hubs in Awka, Anambra
• Partner with local recyclers like Anthophila Limited network
• Onboard corporate sponsors for program funding and material off-take

Our solution addresses gaps in Nigeria's current system where companies like Octopi and LAWMA achieve only 20-30% collection rates due to informal payment systems and mixed waste streams.

### Your solution

**Q2a: What kind of solution are you offering? Is it a technical, managerial, etc. solution? Is your solution about preventing marine litter or reducing marine litter?**

RePlas offers integrated **technical and managerial** solution focused on **prevention** of marine litter.

**Technical:** Blockchain platform provides immutable, transparent tracking of collected plastic, ensuring process integrity and building stakeholder trust. Tokenization drives collection incentives.

**Managerial:** Building new value chain through operational logistics for collection hubs, strategic partnerships with recyclers/corporations, and community engagement programs.

Primary focus is **prevention**—intercepting plastic waste in communities before it reaches rivers/oceans, the most effective intervention point. Unlike existing Nigerian companies like Octopi and LAWMA with 20-30% collection rates, our transparent token system ensures reliable participation.

---

**Q2b: What is the expected (social, environmental and economic) impact of your solution on marine litter? Please quantify where possible.**

**Environmental:** Divert 250,000kg plastic from marine ecosystems in 24 months, addressing Nigeria's status as 10th largest producer of unmanaged plastics (World Bank). Reduce CO2 emissions by 375,000kg through recycling. Improve collection from current 20-30% to 85% in pilot areas.

**Social:** Formalize income for 1,000+ waste pickers excluded from Nigeria's <10% recycling rate (AWAMN data). Train 10,000+ on waste segregation, addressing mixed waste challenges hampering Anthophila Limited/Octopi operations. Reduce flooding by clearing drainage systems.

**Economic:** Generate €100,000 community value through transparent tokens vs. current informal systems. Create certified supply chain addressing quality issues limiting TURN Plastic scalability. Reduce municipal costs, supporting LAWMA's 90% recycling target.

### Stakeholder Integration and cooperation

**Q4a: Please elaborate how your solution will engage/ integrate / benefit local communities and/ or other local stakeholders.**

Unlike existing players who rely on informal payment systems, RePlas creates transparent community value through blockchain-verified transactions.

*   **Addressing Current System Failures:** Where companies like Octopi and LAWMA struggle with inconsistent collection (only 20-30% of Nigeria's waste is properly managed), our token-incentive model ensures reliable community participation through guaranteed, transparent payments.

*   **Filling Institutional Gaps:** WAPAN data shows <10% recycling rates due to mixed waste streams. Our app educates collectors on proper sorting, addressing the core challenge that limits companies like Anthophila Limited's processing efficiency.

*   **Financial Inclusion:** Digital tokens provide banking access to informal waste pickers currently excluded from formal financial systems—unlike cash-only models used by existing recyclers.

*   **Skills Development:** Training on digital literacy and waste segregation prepares communities for Nigeria's emerging circular economy, building capacity beyond current NGO models like TURN Plastic's limited educational programs.

*   **Environmental Justice:** By ensuring fair compensation for waste collection, we address the exploitation of informal workers in the current system while improving local environmental conditions that affect flooding and public health in Awka.
